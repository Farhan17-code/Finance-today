Privacy Policy

Last Updated: October 29, 2025

Summary: Knowledge Hub ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website. Please read this privacy policy carefully. If you do not agree with the terms of this privacy policy, please do not access the site.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Personal Information

We may collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you:

  • Subscribe to our newsletter or mailing list
  • Contact us through our contact forms or email
  • Participate in surveys, contests, or promotions
  • Create an account on our website (if applicable)
  • Comment on our articles or engage with our content

The personal information we may collect includes:

  • Name and contact information (email address, phone number, mailing address)
  • Username and password (if you create an account)
  • Demographic information (age, gender, location)
  • Any other information you choose to provide

1.2 Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our website, we automatically collect certain information about your device and browsing behavior, including:

  • Log Data: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, referring/exit pages, date/time stamps, and clickstream data
  • Device Information: Device type, unique device identifiers, mobile network information
  • Usage Data: Pages viewed, time spent on pages, links clicked, search queries, and other actions taken on our site
  • Location Data: General geographic location based on IP address (city/country level, not precise location)

1.3 Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies, web beacons, pixels, and similar tracking technologies to collect information about your browsing activities. Cookies are small data files stored on your device that help us improve your experience and understand how you interact with our website.

Types of cookies we use:

  • Essential Cookies: Necessary for the website to function properly, enabling core functionality like security, network management, and accessibility
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously (e.g., Google Analytics)
  • Advertising Cookies: Used to deliver relevant advertisements to you and track the effectiveness of advertising campaigns
  • Functionality Cookies: Remember your preferences and choices to provide enhanced, personalized features
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by external services we use on our website, including advertising networks and analytics providers

Managing Cookies: Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies from being set
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

Please note that blocking or deleting cookies may impact your user experience and some features of our website may not function properly.

2. Third-Party Advertising and Google AdSense

2.1 Google AdSense

We use Google AdSense to display advertisements on our website. Google AdSense is an advertising service provided by Google LLC that uses cookies and other tracking technologies to serve ads based on your prior visits to our website and other websites on the internet.

How Google AdSense Works:

  • Google uses cookies to serve ads based on your previous visits to this website and other websites
  • Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit to our site and/or other sites on the Internet
  • You may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings or aboutads.info
  • Google may use the DoubleClick cookie to enable them and their partners to serve ads based on your visits to our site and other sites on the Internet

2.2 Third-Party Ad Networks

In addition to Google AdSense, we may work with other third-party advertising networks and partners who may use cookies and similar technologies to collect information about your activities on our website and other websites to provide you with targeted advertising based on your interests and browsing history.

These third-party ad networks may include:

  • Display advertising networks
  • Retargeting and remarketing services
  • Affiliate marketing partners
  • Social media advertising platforms (Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, etc.)

Each third-party advertiser has its own privacy policy regarding how they collect and use your information. We encourage you to review their privacy policies.

2.3 Your Advertising Choices

You have several options to control personalized advertising:

  • Opt-Out Tools: Visit the Network Advertising Initiative opt-out page or the Digital Advertising Alliance opt-out page
  • Browser Settings: Configure your browser to block third-party cookies
  • Ad Blockers: Use browser extensions that block advertisements
  • Mobile Settings: iOS and Android devices have settings to limit ad tracking
  • Do Not Track: Some browsers have "Do Not Track" features; however, not all advertisers honor these signals
